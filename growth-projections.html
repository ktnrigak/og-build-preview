How to use this tool

What this is: An interactive 36-month financial model for OG Build, a two-sided construction operations platform. It projects user growth and SaaS revenue starting from a single founding contractor, assuming no paid marketing.

How to use it: Tap Conservative, Moderate, or Optimistic to load preset scenarios. Drag any slider to customize your own assumptions. Tap the (?) next to any parameter to see what it means, how it affects growth, and where you have leverage.

What to look for: The viral coefficient (K) below the controls tells you whether the model sustains its own growth. The charts below show cumulative users, monthly recurring revenue, and annual run rate. Every number updates instantly as you change inputs.

Default scenario is Moderate. Scroll through the page top to bottom for the full story: growth, revenue, unit economics.

Unit Economics

What one contractor is worth

These metrics translate the model into per-contractor economics. The key number is the CAC reduction from the viral loop: because viral contractors cost nothing to acquire, every dollar of sales effort is worth more than its face value. ARPU (average revenue per paying user) anchors the revenue story.
The viral loop advantage: Without the flywheel, you pay full CAC for every contractor. With it, your effective acquisition cost drops proportionally to the viral multiplier. The viral subs and contractors are free. Your only marginal cost is Twilio SMS (~$0.008/message).
