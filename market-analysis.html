Reading the Market Sizing

TAM (Total Addressable Market) is the entire construction management software market. If every contractor in the world bought software, this is the total spend.

SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market) is the slice we can actually reach: small and mid-size contractors (5-50 employees) in the U.S. who need project management tools but find enterprise solutions too expensive or complex.

SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market) is what we can realistically capture in the first few years: the GC-to-sub coordination niche where no two-sided solution exists today. This is our starting point.

Communication Chaos

GC-to-sub coordination happens across scattered text threads, phone calls, and emails. Critical updates get lost. Nobody has the full picture.

77% of projects suffer significant delays from poor communication

Cost Overruns Are the Norm

Projects consistently exceed budget because scheduling slips aren't caught early. By the time the GC knows a sub is behind, it's already a cascading delay.

98% of megaprojects go over budget (McKinsey)

Rework From Miscommunication

52% of all rework in construction is caused by poor communication and data management. Subs build the wrong thing because they had outdated information.

$31.3B in annual rework costs in the U.S.

Software Adoption Failure

Existing tools are built for enterprise GCs, not mid-size contractors. They're too expensive, too complex, and field crews refuse to use them. Contractors buy software, struggle for 6 months, and go back to spreadsheets.

70% of contractors have no technology roadmap

Subs Are Second-Class Citizens

Every existing platform is GC-centric. The GC buys the tool, invites the sub, and the sub gets zero independent value. They have no reason to log in, so they don't.

Subs use 37%+ fewer digital tools than GCs

Data Locked in Silos

37% of companies use 4+ unintegrated applications per project. Project data lives in spreadsheets, text threads, email chains, and paper forms across devices.

5.5 hours/week spent just searching for project data

The Solution

OG Build: the first two-sided construction ops platform

Unlike every competitor, OG Build gives both GCs and subcontractors independent value. GCs get project intelligence. Subs get a unified view of all their work. The shared workspace replaces text threads.

Shared Project Views

Zero-Friction Sub Onboarding

Project Health at a Glance

GC dashboard shows red/yellow/green health signals for every project. Overdue tasks, unresponsive subs, and schedule risks surface before they cascade.

Cross-GC View for Subs

Estimates to Projects in One Click

Templates for Speed

The Marketplace Flywheel

OG Build starts as an operations platform. But every GC and sub using it creates operational data: who works well together, who delivers on time, which trades are in demand. Over time, this becomes the construction coordination marketplace: GCs find reliable subs, subs find steady work. The marketplace doesn't need to be built. It emerges from usage.

Existing marketplaces (PlanHub, BuildingConnected) handle the bidding phase only. They have weak lock-in because the relationship only exists during the bid. OG Build owns the execution phase, where both sides depend on the platform daily for months. That's 10x stickier.
